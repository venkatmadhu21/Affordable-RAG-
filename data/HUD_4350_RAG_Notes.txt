
AFFD-1001: HUD 4350 - Extracted Notes

CHAPTERS 1-3: INCOME LIMITS

Income Limits
- Based on the county in which the property is located.
- The Median Family Income level is based on residents in that specific county, so values vary by state/area.
- Updated every year.
- Can be found on HUDUser website.

Important Levels:
- 80% of Median Income Level = Low Income
- 50% of Median Income Level = Very Low Income
- 30% of Median Income Level = Extremely Low Income

NONCITIZENS AND ASSISTANCE

If you are an Ineligible Noncitizen (IN):
- You cannot receive assistance from HUD.
- If you are the only household member, you must pay full market rent.

Mixed Households (eligible + non-eligible members):
- Cannot receive full assistance.
- Assistance is prorated.

Example:
- 2 household members.
- 1 eligible, 1 not eligible.
- Market rent = $1000.
- If both were eligible, HUD would pay $600 assistance.
- Since only 50% are eligible, HUD pays 50% of $600 = $300.
- Household pays $700/month.

CHAPTER 5: CALCULATING TOTAL TENANT PAYMENT (TTP)

Section 8, PAC, PRAC, and RAP

TTP is the greater of:
- 30% of monthly adjusted income.
- 10% of monthly gross income.
- Welfare rent (as-paid localities only).
- $25 minimum rent (Section 8 only).

Rules:
- Section 8, RAP, and PAC applicants can be admitted only if TTP is less than gross rent.
- In PRAC properties, TTP may exceed PRAC operating rent.

CHAPTER 7: EFFECTIVE DATES OF INTERIMS

Goal:
- Give tenants at least 30 days notice when rent increases, provided they reported changes timely.

If rent decreases:
- No 30-day notice required.
- Example:
  - Income change: 8/25
  - Certification effective: 9/1

If rent increases:
- 30-day notice may be required.

If tenant did NOT report timely:
- No notice required.
- Income change 8/25 → 9/1 certification possible.

If tenant DID report timely:
- 30-day notice required.
- Income change 8/25 → 10/1 certification.

GROSS RENT CHANGES (GRC)

- Major source of support calls.
- Usually performed once per year.
- Affects all tenants in the property.
- Changes are done by floorplan.

Utility Allowance / Contract Rent:
- Sometimes only Utility Allowance (UA) or Contract Rent (CR) changes.
- If UA decreases, Tenant Rent (TR) increases.

Tenant Signatures:
- Tenants do not need to sign GRC certification unless Tenant Rent increases.
- Copy of unsigned 50059 must still be provided.

Notice Requirement:
- If tenant rent increases, tenants must receive at least 30 days notice.

Property Impact:
- New rent schedule may become effective before tenants start paying the increased rent.
- Property temporarily absorbs the difference.
- HUD often reimburses the property.

CHAPTER 8: REPAYMENT PLANS

- Properties request funding for upcoming months while corrections are often made retroactively.
- Causes include:
  - Fraud
  - Honest errors
  - Reporting issues

Consequences:
- Tenant may suddenly owe HUD a large amount.

HUD Process:
- HUD immediately collects the money from the property.
- Property then attempts to collect from resident.

Repayment Plans:
- If full collection from tenant is difficult, HUD may allow a repayment plan.
- HUD may reimburse the property for the lost amount.

CHAPTER 9: EIV

Enterprise Income Verification (EIV)

- System used by clients.
- Support teams generally do not have direct access.

Common Scenario:
- Client finds income discrepancy in EIV.
- Requests help correcting interim certification and reporting to HUD.

Occasional Scenario:
- Assisting clients in returning money to HUD due to fines related to EIV misuse.

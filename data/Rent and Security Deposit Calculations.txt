Basic Rent Calculation
Basic rent is set by program guidelines and property operating costs. It represents the minimum amount required for a unit and serves as a baseline for rent calculations.

Residents may pay basic rent if their income is above the threshold for assistance.


Tenant Contribution Calculation
The tenant contribution is typically a percentage of adjusted income, often 30%. This amount is compared to the basic rent, and the resident pays the higher of the two, unless rental assistance applies.

This ensures affordability while supporting property operations.


Impact of Rental Assistance
Rental assistance covers the difference between the tenant contribution and the basic rent. Eligible residents pay only what they can afford, and the subsidy makes up the rest.

This support helps keep housing affordable for low-income households.


Adjustments for Special Populations
Elderly and disabled households may qualify for additional deductions or lower rent contributions. These adjustments recognize the unique financial challenges faced by these groups.

Staff should be familiar with all allowable deductions and apply them consistently.

Restrictions on Increasing Deposits
Security deposits cannot exceed the greater of one month’s rent or the basic rent. This limit ensures deposits remain reasonable and affordable for residents.

Always check program guidelines for any local or property-specific variations.

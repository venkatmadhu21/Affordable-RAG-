Who Makes Up an Eligible Household?
Eligibility is not just about income—it's also about who is in the household. Expand each section to learn more.

Families
Families may include parents, children, and other dependents living together. Eligibility is based on total household income and all members must be listed on the application.

Special consideration is given to family size and composition when determining eligibility and rental assistance needs.


Elderly Households
An elderly household includes at least one member who is 62 years or older or has a qualifying disability. These households may be eligible for additional deductions or priority in some programs.

Documentation of age or disability status is required for verification.


Individuals with Disabilities
Households with one or more members who have a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits major life activities may qualify as disabled households.

Verification of disability is only required if it affects eligibility or program benefits.


Farmworker Households
Farmworker households must receive a substantial portion of their income from farm labor. These households are eligible for specific rural housing programs designed to support agricultural workers.

Proof of employment and income from farm work is necessary for eligibility.

Eligibility for rural housing programs is based on both income limits and the makeup of the household. Different programs each have their own specific requirements.

Despite these differences, all programs require applicants to stay within certain income limits and to provide documentation for every member of the household.

Some programs may give preference to specific groups, such as elderly individuals, people with disabilities, or families of farmworkers. Property staff need to understand the distinct eligibility rules for each program to ensure that applications are processed fairly and correctly.

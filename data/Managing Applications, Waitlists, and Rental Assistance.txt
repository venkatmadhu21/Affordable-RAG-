Waitlist Setup and Maintenance
Waitlists can be maintained in written, electronic, or spreadsheet formats. They must be updated regularly to reflect current applicant status and order.

Ensure the waitlist is accessible for review and audit, and that it accurately reflects all applicants.


Placing Applicants on the Waitlist
Every application received—regardless of completeness or eligibility—must be added to the waitlist. This ensures equal treatment and transparency for all applicants.

Applicants should be listed in the order their applications are received.


Notification Requirements and Timelines
Applicants must be notified in writing within ten calendar days of a completed application. Notifications should clearly state whether the applicant has been selected, waitlisted, or rejected.

Timely communication helps applicants understand their status and next steps.


Handling Incomplete or Ineligible Applications
Even incomplete or ineligible applications must be logged and placed on the waitlist. Applicants should be notified of missing information or reasons for ineligibility and given the opportunity to provide additional documentation if appropriate.

Clear documentation of these actions is essential for compliance.

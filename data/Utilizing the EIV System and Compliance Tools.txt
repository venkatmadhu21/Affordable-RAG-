The Enterprise Income Verification (EIV) system is a secure, web-based tool used in HUD rental assistance programs to verify resident income and employment information. EIV pulls data from multiple sources to help property staff confirm that residents are reporting their income accurately. By using EIV, onsite teams can reduce errors, prevent fraud, and ensure that rent subsidies are calculated correctly. The system is a critical part of the compliance process, supporting both initial certifications and annual recertifications.

Using EIV Reports for Verification
EIV reports are essential tools for verifying resident information and maintaining compliance. Each report type serves a specific purpose in daily operations. Explore the sections below to learn how to use each report effectively.


Income Reports
Income reports provide a summary of all employment and benefit income for each resident. These reports help staff verify that the income reported by residents matches official records.

Reviewing income reports regularly helps prevent errors and ensures accurate rent calculations.


Benefit Reports
Benefit reports detail federal benefits such as Social Security, Medicare, and Medicaid received by residents. These reports support the verification of non-employment income sources.

Using benefit reports ensures that all sources of income are included in the certification process.


Discrepancy Reports
Discrepancy reports highlight differences between resident-reported income and data found in EIV. These reports alert staff to potential issues that require further investigation.

Timely review and resolution of discrepancies are critical for maintaining program compliance and avoiding penalties.


Regularly reviewing EIV reports and addressing any discrepancies helps prevent compliance issues and ensures that tenants receive the appropriate assistance.
